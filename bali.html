Contributed by - Ananya Chakraborty

Bali is a popular holiday destination for people around the world and it is very obvious why! From the list of numerous beaches, temples, activities, delicious food- there is so much to see and explore.

Going to Bali was always a dream for me, and so I decided to spend my birthday with friends in Bali. Bali has an activity for every soul, but I decided to go for the adventure activities and the party side of the island. The best time to visit Bali is during the dry season i.e May to October, but since my birthday was in November, I went for that month. Usually a rainy season at that time, it was surprisingly warm and dry during that time. But if you go for a dry season, you dont have to worry about the rains soiling your plans.

The only problem might be that it would be too crowded, especially in the popular tourist destinations and also flights and hotels would be more expensive. But between November to April, its the cheapest time to go, so I got pretty cheap deals and also no rains thanks to my luck.

IMPORTANT INFORMATION

Total expense of the trip – 50,000 Rs for 8 days

Language – The main languages are Indonesian, Balinese and English

Currency – The currency is Indonesian Rupiah. 1 INR equals to 197 rupiah. 1 USD equals to 14,792 rupiah.

ATM – There are plenty of ATMs everywhere except the islands like Nusa Penida, Nusa Lembongan, Gili islands.

Charging cable – One of the issues I faced was that the plugs in Bali are different than that of India. So, it is advised to carry an universal adapter, as the local markets there know this and thus sell low quality adapters at very high price.

Safety – Bali is very safe for solo female travelers, couples and group of females. There is safety even at night and in places of solitude. I didn’t find any issues with safety there, though past 8-9 pm at night, all the shops start getting closed.

Flights – The flights from India to Bali are quite reasonable. I booked through Malindo air and my return airfare was 18,000 Rs.

Visa – There is visa on arrival in Bali and it is free of cost. Also the queue for immigration is usually very small.

Sim card – There are plenty of simcards in the airport itself post the immigration, you can choose according to your needs and prices. WE got a sim for IDR 100,000.

Seminyak and Canggu

There are no direct flights from India to Bali, so we had a layover at Malaysia for 2 hours. We reached at Ngurah Rai International Airport in the morning at 11:30 am, the queue for immigration was very small. It was easy to find a cab from the airport at a very reasonable price. There is no Uber in Bali, but you can find a Grab taxi, or Go-jek motorcycle taxi. We took the Grab for IDR 63000 to Seminyak- which was our first destination. It was 15 mins from the airport. We had taken a private pool villa in “New Pondak Sara Villas”. The price per night for a room with private swimming pool and breakfast was 9,08,000 IDR. The hotel was full of greenery and each villa was very spacious with hall, room, kitchen, a very large bathroom with bathtub, and a swimming pool. The staff was very hospitable and food was delicious.

After freshening up, we started to explore. Seminyak was the hub for the best beach clubs, best shopping malls and areas and things were available really cheap there. It was also close to Caggnu, where we wanted to explore the famous Finn’s beach club and Potato Head beach club. It was in the center to everything and also close to the airport. The best way to explore Bali is through a motorcycle. So we went to a bike rent shop near our hotel and rented motorbikes at the price of 1,00,000 per day. Bike rentals are everywhere, you just have to bargain to get a better price. After taking the bike, we explored the shopping areas of Seminyak, the Seminyak beach and headed to the famous Finn’s beach club in Caggnu. It was a 20 min drive. It was one of the best beach clubs with an infinity pool overlooking the ocean.

There is an no entry charge, but you have to pay for a locker, approx IDR 50,000. You would need the locker as you cant swim in your regular clothes and need your swimsuits. The changing rooms are right adjacent and very clean. You can alternatively book single bed, deluxe bed, lagoon deluxe bed, lagoon party bed for the whole day. You can also go to Finn’s VIP beach club which is just adjacent and is just a little bit expensive and more luxurious. We didn’t book any bed for the day as we wanted to spend 2-3 hours there and enjoy and later proceed to explore other places. Finn’s had the best food, a bit expensive but the price and taste was worth it. Alcohol was a little extra charged than outside but well, you had an option to enjoy it in a pool overlooking the ocean. So it was beautiful. There are 3 pools with an amazing DJ for dance and enjoying the food.

After Finn’s, we went to the Seminyak beach and explored the famous La Plancha beach club. This place was really famous among tourists and I had always seen its pictures in various social medias. It’s an amazing place to sit on a bean bag and enjoy the sunset with some food and a beer. Due to its popularity, it gets hard to get a seat in the front rows, but the views are worth it, also the food is not that expensive.

Ubud

The next day, we woke up early morning for the Mount Batur trek. Its a volcanic mountain and the sunrise from there is awesome. You can book it locally or online, there are tons of operators for the trek. Post the trek, we went to Ubud. Our first spot was the Karsa Spa. This is one of the most famous spa in Bali, for the traditional Balinese massage. Tip- It is impossible to get an appointment for spa there due to its popularity, you have to book it a month or two ago.

The spa was perfect to remove our tiredness from the trek. Spa for two costed IDR 659,200 including a flower bath at the end. It was really worth it. They ask you to choose oil according to your preference for the spa. The spa was so soothing and the flower bath was very beautiful, it was good for skin as well. We came out glowing from the spa and then headed to Ubud for some sightseeing.

As I had mentioned, we kept our trip focused on adventure activities and unique experiences and less on overcrowded famous tourist spots. Ubud was full of traditional temples and palaces to see. After some shopping at the Ubud market and some sightseeing, we came back in the evening to our hotel in Seminyak, and went to Kuta-Legian to explore the nightlife there. Legian was 15 minute drive and was a party hub.

Kuta-Legian and Uluwatu

That night we explored the Legian street which had various pubs and nightclubs. It was a nice party atmosphere. The next morning we woke up early in the morning and went to explore the Potato Head Beach Club at Caggnu. It was another famous beach club in the area.

An obvious must visit in Uluwatu is the temple with the same name. We visited on our first morning in Uluwatu, thinking it would be ideal to cross this one off the list. You are supposed to go at sunset to witness the kecak dance, but we didn’t. Instead we opted to go when we were sweating our asses off in the midday heat. Anyway, you should probably visit at sundown – but that’s also when all the tour busses arrive.

There is a saying in Bali: “We have no art. We do everything as beautifully as possible.” This reflects my philosophy of practice. I try to remember daily what a gift it is to have the privilege of living in this wondrous world. -Sam Keen
